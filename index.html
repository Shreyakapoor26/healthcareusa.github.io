Healthcare Access, Affordability, and Outcomes Across the United States: A Narrative Visualization for Public Health Policy Makers

Access to healthcare is a fundamental right for all individuals, but unfortunately, it remains a significant problem in the United States. Healthcare costs and insurance availability vary greatly across states, making it difficult for individuals to access the care they need. Moreover, health outcomes vary by state, highlighting the need for a comprehensive understanding of healthcare access and outcomes across the country. In recent years, the cost of healthcare has also become a significant barrier to accessing care, with many individuals delaying or forgoing necessary medical services due to high costs.

This project aims to address these challenges by creating a narrative visualization that provides users with information on healthcare access, medical debt, and health outcomes across states. By doing so, the project hopes to promote greater transparency in healthcare data and enable policymakers to make informed decisions regarding healthcare policy.
